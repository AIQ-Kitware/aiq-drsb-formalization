\section*{Reference being distilled}
This note distills Girsanov, \emph{On Transforming a Certain Class of Stochastic Processes by Absolutely Continuous Substitution of Measures}, Theory of Probability and Its Applications 5(3), 285--301, 1960 \cite{Girsanov1960}.

\section*{Role in the formalization}
This is the classical source for changing drift by an absolutely continuous change of path measure.  It is relevant to dynamic Schr\"odinger bridge/SOC arguments where controlled diffusions are expressed as tilted Brownian path measures and relative entropy becomes quadratic control energy.

\begin{distillation}
\item \textbf{Setup.}  Start with a Wiener process $W$ under $P$ and an adapted drift/control process $u_t$ satisfying integrability conditions.
\item \textbf{Exponential martingale.}  The Radon--Nikodym derivative is given by a stochastic exponential
\[
  Z_T=\exp\left(\int_0^T u_t\cdot dW_t - \frac12\int_0^T \|u_t\|^2dt\right),
\]
under appropriate conditions ensuring $Z$ is a martingale.
\item \textbf{New Brownian motion.}  Under $Q$ defined by $dQ=Z_TdP$, the process
\[
  \widetilde W_t=W_t-\int_0^t u_sds
\]
is Brownian.  Thus adding drift under one measure is equivalent to changing measure.
\item \textbf{Entropy/energy identity.}  In the regular case,
\[
  \KL(Q\mid P)=\frac12\E_Q\int_0^T\|u_t\|^2dt.
\]
This identity is the analytic bridge from relative entropy over path measures to stochastic optimal control cost.
\end{distillation}
