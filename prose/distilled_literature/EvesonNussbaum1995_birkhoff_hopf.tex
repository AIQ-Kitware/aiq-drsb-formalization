\section*{Reference being distilled}
This note distills Eveson and Nussbaum, \emph{An Elementary Proof of the Birkhoff--Hopf Theorem}, Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society 117(1), 31--55, 1995 \cite{EvesonNussbaum1995}.

\section*{Role in the formalization}
This is a general cone-theoretic source for Birkhoff--Hopf contraction.  It is broader than the finite matrix formalization, but it can justify the abstract contraction principle if the Lean development later generalizes from finite vectors to ordered vector spaces or cones of functions.

\begin{distillation}
\item \textbf{Cone order and oscillation.}  In an ordered vector space with cone $C$, compare positive elements $x,y$ by the least and greatest scalars $\alpha,\beta$ such that $\alpha x\le y\le \beta x$.  The logarithm of $\beta/\alpha$ is Hilbert's projective metric.
\item \textbf{Positive homogeneous maps.}  If a linear map sends the interior of one cone into the interior of another with finite projective diameter, then it contracts Hilbert metric by a coefficient strictly below one.
\item \textbf{Two-dimensional reduction.}  The proof reduces the contraction inequality to the cone generated by two elements and then to the standard positive quadrant in $\R^2$, where the estimate is an elementary calculus inequality.
\item \textbf{Convergence consequence.}  Once a positive operator is a strict projective contraction on an invariant normalized slice, Banach-style iteration yields convergence of rays and hence asymptotic regularity.
\end{distillation}
