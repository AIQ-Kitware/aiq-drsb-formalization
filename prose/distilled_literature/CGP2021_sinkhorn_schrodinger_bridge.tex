\section*{Core finite Schr\"odinger/Sinkhorn argument}
Let $X=\{1,\dots,n\}$, let $p,q$ be strictly positive probability vectors, and let $G=(G_{ij})$ be a strictly positive reference kernel.  The finite Schr\"odinger bridge problem is
\[
  \min_{\pi\in\Pcal(X\times X)} \KL(\pi\mid G)
  \quad\text{subject to}\quad
  \sum_j \pi_{ij}=p_i,\qquad \sum_i \pi_{ij}=q_j .
\]
Up to harmless normalization of $G$, the optimal coupling has the Gibbs/Sinkhorn form
\[
  \pi^*_{ij}=a_i G_{ij} b_j,
\]
where $a,b>0$ solve the two marginal equations
\[
  a_i \sum_j G_{ij} b_j=p_i,\qquad
  b_j \sum_i a_i G_{ij}=q_j .
\]
The pair $(a,b)$ is unique up to the scalar transformation $(a,b)\mapsto(ca,c^{-1}b)$.

\begin{distillation}
\item \textbf{Convex variational reduction.}  Relative entropy is strictly convex on the positive support of $G$.  Introducing Lagrange multipliers for the two marginals gives
\[
  \log(\pi_{ij}/G_{ij})+1+\alpha_i+\beta_j=0,
\]
so any interior minimizer has multiplicative form $a_iG_{ij}b_j$.
\item \textbf{Schr\"odinger equations.}  Substituting the multiplicative ansatz into the marginal constraints produces the nonlinear system above.  The system is invariant under reciprocal scaling of the two potentials.
\item \textbf{Sinkhorn/Fortet iteration.}  Alternately enforce the left and right marginal equations by updating one potential from the other.  In finite positive dimension, the maps are order-preserving and homogeneous after quotienting by the scalar gauge.
\item \textbf{Projective convergence.}  The classical convergence mechanism is not ordinary Euclidean contraction of the potentials themselves, because potentials are defined up to scale.  The correct object is the ray in the positive cone.  Positivity of $G$ gives finite projective diameter, and Birkhoff/Hilbert contraction gives convergence of projective classes.  This is the source of the projective-lag statements still being formalized.
\item \textbf{Gauge closure.}  Once the projective ray is controlled, a normalization condition such as fixed total mass of one potential fixes the scalar and gives ordinary coordinate convergence.
\end{distillation}
