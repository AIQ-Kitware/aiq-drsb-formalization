\section{Formalized statement}
For the iid standard Gaussian law on $\R^{\mathbb N}$ and deterministic shift $c$:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item if $\sum_n c_n^2<\infty$, the shifted law is absolutely continuous and
$\KL=\frac12\sum_n c_n^2$;
\item if the square sum diverges, the KL divergence is $\Top$;
\item finite KL is equivalent to square summability.
\end{itemize}

\section{Proof architecture}
Lean proves finite-prefix density formulas, an $L^2$ bound for the density martingale, absolute continuity of the projective limit, finite-prefix KL formulas, and the infinite branch.  This is a genuine end-to-end model rather than an assembly theorem.
