\section*{Reference being distilled}
This note distills Yeh, \emph{Transformation of Conditional Wiener Integrals under Translation and the Cameron--Martin Translation Theorem}, Tohoku Mathematical Journal 30(4), 505--515, 1978 \cite{Yeh1978}.

\section*{Role in the formalization}
This is a specialized source for conditional Wiener integrals under endpoint conditioning.  It is relevant if the project formalizes conditional bridge/path integral formulas rather than only finite-state Sinkhorn systems.

\begin{distillation}
\item \textbf{Conditional Wiener integral.}  The paper treats conditional expectations of Wiener-integrable functionals given the endpoint map $X[x]=x(t)$.
\item \textbf{Translation path.}  Let $x_0$ be absolutely continuous with square-integrable derivative.  The Cameron--Martin density supplies the Radon--Nikodym correction under $x\mapsto x+x_0$.
\item \textbf{Endpoint shift.}  Conditioning on $x(t)=\xi$ interacts with translation by replacing the endpoint condition with $\xi-x_0(t)$ and multiplying by the corresponding exponential density factors.
\item \textbf{Use in bridges.}  This is a path-integral route to Brownian bridge translation identities, which in turn underlie Gaussian bridge computations.
\end{distillation}
