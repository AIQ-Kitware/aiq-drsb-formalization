\section{Literature result}
For probability measures $\mu\ll\nu$ and a measurable potential $f$, the change-of-measure inequality is
\[
 \int f\,d\mu\le \KL(\mu\Vert\nu)+\log\int e^f\,d\nu,
\]
and the corresponding supremum is attained at the Gibbs tilt of $\nu$ by $f$.

\section{Formalized proof}
Lean tilts $\nu$ by $f$, applies nonnegativity of relative entropy against the tilted measure, rewrites the tilted log-likelihood ratio, and rearranges.  Equality is proved by direct evaluation at the tilt.  The result is consumed by the Sinkhorn-DRO weak and converse Lagrangian theorems.

\section{Comparison}
\begin{comparison}
Measure class & Probability measures, matching the primary DRO application.\\
Absolute continuity & Explicit $\mu\ll\nu$.\\
Integrability & $f$ and the log-likelihood ratio are explicitly integrable under $\mu$; $e^f$ is integrable under $\nu$.\\
Value type & Real equality through \texttt{(klDiv ...).toReal}.\\
Optimizer & The tilted measure is explicitly constructed and proved optimal.\\
Equality characterization & Attainment at the tilt is proved; uniqueness is not packaged as a standalone theorem.\\
\end{comparison}
