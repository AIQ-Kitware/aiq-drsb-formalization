\section*{Role in the formalization}
This is a modern algorithmic stochastic-control source for fine-tuning generative dynamics.  It is relevant to applied/generative-model claims but not to the current finite Sinkhorn proof core.

\begin{distillation}
\item \textbf{Reward fine-tuning as SOC.}  The paper casts reward fine-tuning of flow/diffusion generative models as a stochastic optimal-control problem.
\item \textbf{Memoryless noise condition.}  A central mathematical point is that the noise schedule must be chosen so that the control problem accounts correctly for dependence between noise and generated samples.
\item \textbf{Adjoint regression.}  The SOC optimality system is converted into an adjoint/matching regression objective, avoiding direct dynamic-programming over full histories.
\item \textbf{Formalization relevance.}  The core proof themes are controlled diffusion, adjoint equations, and change-of-measure/SOC identities, which connect more to Girsanov-style path measures than to finite matrix scaling.
\end{distillation}
