\section{Literature comparison}
Data processing is the standard convex-Jensen theorem for $f$-divergences.  Lean proves it from Mathlib's Radon--Nikodym derivative of a pushforward, conditional expectation, Jensen's inequality for the KL generator, and the integral representation of KL.  The kernel chain rule extends Mathlib's product/comp-product infrastructure to the exact conditional form needed by the continuum energy decomposition.

\begin{comparison}
Data processing & Fully proved, including an $\ENN$ inequality and finite-real corollary.\\
Kernel chain rule & Proved under countable-generation and absolute-continuity/finiteness assumptions required by Mathlib's RN-derivative API.\\
Projection exhaustion & Upper bounds are general; equality requires a sequence of projections whose KL values converge appropriately.\\
Primary client & \Lean{ChenGeorgiouPavon2021.dynamic_eq_static_SB} and the energy-identity decomposition.\\
Literature source & Classical information theory rather than one unique paper; local source attribution should remain theorem-specific.\\
\end{comparison}

\section{Nonclaims}
This family is not a general formalization of all $f$-divergence data processing, nor does it automatically prove path-space KL exhaustion for arbitrary coordinate systems.  The latter requires a Borel-generation or density-martingale theorem specialized to the chosen path carrier.
