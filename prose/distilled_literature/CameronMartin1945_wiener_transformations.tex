\section*{Reference being distilled}
This note distills Cameron and Martin, \emph{Transformations of Wiener Integrals under a General Class of Linear Transformations}, Transactions of the American Mathematical Society 58(2), 184--219, 1945 \cite{CameronMartin1945}.

\section*{Role in the formalization}
This is a primary Wiener-integral transformation source.  The uploaded paper is about a broad class of linear transformations of Wiener space, with translation results used as background.  It supports continuum Gaussian-measure/quasi-invariance arguments adjacent to Schr\"odinger bridge path-space proofs.

\begin{distillation}
\item \textbf{Wiener space.}  Work on continuous paths $x:[0,1]\to\R$ with $x(0)=0$ under Wiener measure.
\item \textbf{Linear transformation.}  Consider transformations of the form
\[
  y(t)=x(t)+\int_0^1 K(t,s)x(s)\,ds
\]
under analytic conditions on the kernel $K$ ensuring invertibility and measurability.
\item \textbf{Jacobian/Radon--Nikodym correction.}  The Wiener integral of a functional after transformation equals an integral over the original path with a determinant factor and an exponential quadratic correction.
\item \textbf{Connection to Cameron--Martin.}  Translation by an absolutely continuous path with square-integrable derivative is the simpler, now-standard quasi-invariance theorem: the translated measure is absolutely continuous and has exponential linear/quadratic density.
\end{distillation}
