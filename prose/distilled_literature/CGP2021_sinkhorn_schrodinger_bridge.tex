\section{The dynamic Schr\"odinger problem}
Let $R$ be a Markov path measure on a path space $\Omega$ over $[0,1]$, with endpoint joint law
$R_{01}$ and bridge disintegration
\[
 R(d\omega)=R^{xy}(d\omega)R_{01}(dx,dy).
\]
Given endpoint laws $\rho_0,\rho_1$, the Schr\"odinger bridge problem is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-dynamic}
 \inf\{\KL(P\|R):P_0=\rho_0, P_1=\rho_1\}.
\end{equation}
Disintegrating $P=P^{xy}P_{01}$ and applying the entropy chain rule gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-chain}
 \KL(P\|R)
 =\KL(P_{01}\|R_{01})
 +\int\KL(P^{xy}\|R^{xy})P_{01}(dx,dy).
\end{equation}
The second term is nonnegative and vanishes exactly when $P^{xy}=R^{xy}$ for
$P_{01}$-almost every $(x,y)$.  Therefore the dynamic problem is equivalent to the static endpoint
problem
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-static}
 \inf\{\KL(\pi\|R_{01}):\pi_0=\rho_0, \pi_1=\rho_1\},
\end{equation}
and the optimal path law is obtained by mixing the reference bridges with the optimal endpoint
coupling:
\[
 P^*(d\omega)=R^{xy}(d\omega)\pi^*(dx,dy).
\]
This is the dynamic--static equivalence.

\section{The Schr\"odinger system}
Assume $R_{01}$ has a strictly positive kernel representation
\[
 R_{01}(dx,dy)=r_0(dx)g(x,y)r_1(dy).
\]
The Euler--Lagrange equation for \eqref{eq:CGP-static} gives
\[
 \log\frac{d\pi^*}{dR_{01}}(x,y)=a(x)+b(y),
\]
so, after exponentiating,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-factorization}
 \pi^*(dx,dy)=\widehat\varphi(x)g(x,y)\varphi(y)
 r_0(dx)r_1(dy).
\end{equation}
The marginal constraints become
\begin{align}
 \widehat\varphi(x)
 \int g(x,y)\varphi(y)r_1(dy)&=\frac{d\rho_0}{dr_0}(x),\label{eq:CGP-sch1}\\
 \varphi(y)
 \int g(x,y)\widehat\varphi(x)r_0(dx)&=\frac{d\rho_1}{dr_1}(y).\label{eq:CGP-sch2}
\end{align}
The pair is determined only up to the gauge
$(\widehat\varphi,\varphi)\mapsto(a\widehat\varphi,a^{-1}\varphi)$.
Strict convexity of relative entropy gives uniqueness of the coupling, and hence uniqueness of the
potential rays.

\section{Fortet iteration}
Eliminate $\widehat\varphi$ from \eqref{eq:CGP-sch1}--\eqref{eq:CGP-sch2}.  In density notation,
Fortet's fixed-point map is
\[
 \mathcal C(h)(x)
 :=\int g(x,y)
 \frac{\rho_1(y)}
 {\int g(z,y)\rho_0(z)/h(z)\,dz}dy.
\]
A fixed point $h=\mathcal C(h)$ yields
$\widehat\varphi=\rho_0/h$ and then $\varphi$ from the second denominator.  The map is
order-preserving and positively homogeneous.  In the continuous setting it need not preserve a cone
with nonempty interior, so Fortet's proof uses truncations and monotone approximation rather than a
direct contraction theorem.

\section{Girsanov and stochastic control}
Take as reference a diffusion
\[
 dX_t=f(t,X_t)dt+\sigma(t,X_t)\,dW_t,
 \qquad a=\sigma\sigma^\top.
\]
A controlled diffusion with the same noise channels has drift $f+\sigma u$.  Under the usual
absolute-continuity and square-integrability assumptions, Girsanov's theorem gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-girsanov}
 \KL(P^u\|R)
 =\KL(P^u_0\|R_0)
 +\frac12\E_{P^u}\int_0^1\|u(t,X_t)\|^2dt.
\end{equation}
When the initial law is fixed, minimizing path-space entropy with prescribed endpoints is therefore
equivalent to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-control}
 \inf_{(\rho,u)}
 \int_0^1\int\frac12\|u(t,x)\|^2\rho(t,x)dxdt
\end{equation}
subject to the controlled Fokker--Planck equation and the two endpoint densities.  This is the
stochastic-control formulation of the bridge.

\section{Generalized control with a state potential}
The paper also studies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-generalized-control}
 \inf_{(\rho,u)}
 \int_0^1\int
 \left(\frac12\|u\|^2+V(t,x)\right)\rho(t,x)dxdt
\end{equation}
subject to
\[
 \partial_t\rho+
 \nabla\cdot((f+\sigma u)\rho)
 =\frac12\sum_{i,j}\partial_{ij}(a_{ij}\rho),
 \qquad \rho(0)=\rho_0, \rho(1)=\rho_1.
\]
Introduce a Lagrange multiplier $\lambda(t,x)$ for the Fokker--Planck equation and integrate by parts.
Pointwise minimization in $u$ gives
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-opt-control}
 u^*=\sigma^\top\nabla\lambda.
\end{equation}
The remaining stationarity condition is the Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman equation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-HJB}
 \partial_t\lambda+f\cdot\nabla\lambda
 +\frac12\sum_{i,j}a_{ij}\partial_{ij}\lambda
 +\frac12\nabla\lambda\cdot a\nabla\lambda-V=0.
\end{equation}
If $\rho$ solves the controlled Fokker--Planck equation with drift
$f+a\nabla\lambda$ and satisfies the terminal marginal, then
$(\rho,u^*)$ is optimal.  Indeed, completing the square in the integrated Lagrangian shows that any
other feasible $u$ has excess cost
\[
 \frac12\int_0^1\int
 \|u-\sigma^\top\nabla\lambda\|^2\rho\,dx\,dt.
\]

The Hopf--Cole transform $\varphi=e^\lambda$ linearizes
\eqref{eq:CGP-HJB}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-forward-potential}
 \partial_t\varphi+f\cdot\nabla\varphi
 +\frac12\sum_{i,j}a_{ij}\partial_{ij}\varphi=V\varphi.
\end{equation}
Writing $\widehat\varphi=\rho/\varphi$ gives the adjoint equation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-backward-potential}
 \partial_t\widehat\varphi+
 \nabla\cdot(f\widehat\varphi)
 -\frac12\sum_{i,j}\partial_{ij}(a_{ij}\widehat\varphi)
 =-V\widehat\varphi.
\end{equation}
The nonlinear coupling is entirely in the split boundary conditions
\[
 \varphi(0,\cdot)\widehat\varphi(0,\cdot)=\rho_0,
 \qquad
 \varphi(1,\cdot)\widehat\varphi(1,\cdot)=\rho_1.
\]
This is the generalized Schr\"odinger system, and the optimal control is
$u^*=\sigma^\top\nabla\log\varphi$.

\section{Entropic optimal transport and free energy}
For a Brownian reference with variance parameter $\varepsilon$, the endpoint kernel has density
proportional to
$\exp(-\|x-y\|^2/(2\varepsilon))$.  Expanding
$\KL(\pi\|R_{01})$ separates a quadratic transport term from the entropy of $\pi$:
\[
 \varepsilon\KL(\pi\|R_{01})
 =\frac12\int\|x-y\|^2\pi(dx,dy)
 +\varepsilon\KL(\pi\|r_0\otimes r_1)+\text{constants fixed by the marginals}.
\]
Therefore the static Schr\"odinger bridge is exactly entropy-regularized optimal transport.  As
$\varepsilon\downarrow0$, the entropy term vanishes at leading order and the problem approaches
quadratic optimal transport; dynamically, the controlled diffusion formulation approaches the
Benamou--Brenier kinetic action.

\section{Finite Schr\"odinger system}
Let $G=(g_{ij})$ be an $n\times n$ matrix with all $g_{ij}>0$, and let $p,q$ be positive probability
vectors.  Seek positive vectors
$\varphi_0,\widehat\varphi_0,\varphi_1,\widehat\varphi_1$ satisfying
\begin{align}
 \varphi_0&=G\varphi_1, &
 \widehat\varphi_1&=G^\top\widehat\varphi_0,\label{eq:CGP-linear-steps}\\
 \varphi_0\odot\widehat\varphi_0&=p, &
 \varphi_1\odot\widehat\varphi_1&=q.\label{eq:CGP-boundary-products}
\end{align}
The associated optimal coupling is
\[
 \pi_{ij}^*=\widehat\varphi_{0,i}g_{ij}\varphi_{1,j}.
\]
It has marginals $p,q$, and strict convexity of finite relative entropy makes it the unique minimizer.

Define linear positive maps
$\mathcal E(x)=Gx$ and $\mathcal E^\dagger(x)=G^\top x$, and componentwise inversion/scaling maps
\[
 \mathcal D_0(x)=p/x,
 \qquad
 \mathcal D_1(x)=q/x.
\]
The fixed-point map on $\varphi_0$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CGP-finite-map}
 \mathcal C
 =\mathcal E\circ\mathcal D_1\circ
  \mathcal E^\dagger\circ\mathcal D_0.
\end{equation}

\section{Hilbert projective contraction}
For positive vectors $x,y$, Hilbert's projective metric is
\[
 d_H(x,y)=
 \log\frac{\max_i x_i/y_i}{\min_i x_i/y_i}.
\]
It is invariant under positive rescaling.  Componentwise inversion and multiplication by a fixed
positive vector are isometries.  A positive linear map $A$ has finite projective diameter
\[
 \Delta(A)=\sup_{x,y>0}d_H(Ax,Ay)<\infty
\]
when all matrix entries are positive, and Birkhoff--Bushell theory gives a contraction coefficient
strictly less than one.  Hence both $\mathcal E$ and $\mathcal E^\dagger$ contract rays, while
$\mathcal D_0$ and $\mathcal D_1$ preserve projective distance.  Their composition
\eqref{eq:CGP-finite-map} is therefore a strict projective contraction.

The ray space of the positive cone is complete.  Banach's fixed-point theorem gives a unique fixed ray
$[\varphi_0^*]$ and projective convergence of every positive iteration
$\varphi_0^{k+1}=\mathcal C(\varphi_0^k)$.  Positive homogeneity implies
$\mathcal C(\varphi_0^*)=\alpha\varphi_0^*$ for some $\alpha>0$.  The marginal-product equations
force $\alpha=1$: summing the induced coupling by rows and columns gives the same total mass, so the
scale multiplier cannot differ from one.  Thus there is an actual fixed vector after normalization.
Recovering the other three potentials through the four maps gives a solution of
\eqref{eq:CGP-linear-steps}--\eqref{eq:CGP-boundary-products}.

\begin{theorem}[Finite existence, uniqueness, and convergence]
For every strictly positive finite kernel $G$ and positive probability vectors $p,q$, the finite
Schr\"odinger system has a positive solution.  The potential pair is unique up to reciprocal scaling,
and the Fortet--IPF--Sinkhorn iteration converges projectively from every positive initialization.  A
fixed gauge converts projective convergence into ordinary coordinate convergence.
\end{theorem}

\section{The common mathematical chain}
The paper's central equivalences can be summarized without identifying the objects:
\[
 \boxed{
 \begin{array}{c}
 \text{minimum path-space relative entropy}\\
 \Updownarrow\\
 \text{minimum endpoint relative entropy}\\
 \Updownarrow\\
 \text{Schr\"odinger factorization and scaling equations}\\
 \Updownarrow\\
 \text{stochastic control with quadratic energy}\\
 \Updownarrow\\
 \text{entropic optimal transport/free-energy minimization}.
 \end{array}}
\]
The finite positive case adds a constructive solution through a contraction on projective rays.
