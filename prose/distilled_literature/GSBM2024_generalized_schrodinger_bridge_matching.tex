\section{The generalized bridge problem}
Consider controlled diffusions on $[0,1]$,
\[
 dX_t=u_t(X_t)\,dt+\sigma\,dW_t,
 \qquad X_0\sim\mu, X_1\sim\nu,
\]
with time marginals $p_t$.  The generalized Schr\"odinger bridge problem is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:GSBM-main}
 \inf_u\int_0^1
 \E_{p_t}\left[\frac12\|u_t(X_t)\|^2+V_t(X_t)\right]dt
\end{equation}
subject to the Fokker--Planck equation
\[
 \partial_t p_t=-\nabla\cdot(p_tu_t)+\frac{\sigma^2}{2}\Delta p_t,
 \qquad p_0=\mu, p_1=\nu.
\]
The state cost $V_t$ may encode geometry, interactions, obstacles, or rewards.  When $V_t=0$ this
reduces to the classical Schr\"odinger bridge kinetic-energy problem.

\section{Stage 1: minimizing over drifts at fixed marginals}
Fix a smooth positive probability path $(p_t)_{0\le t\le1}$.  Among all vector fields producing this
path through the Fokker--Planck equation, the kinetic energy is uniquely minimized by a gradient
field $u_t^*=\nabla s_t^*$.  The potential is characterized variationally by the entropic action
functional
\[
 \mathcal L_{\rm implicit}(s)
 =\E_\mu[s_0(X_0)]-E_\nu[s_1(X_1)]
 +\int_0^1\E_{p_t}
 \left[\partial_ts_t+\frac12\|\nabla s_t\|^2
        +\frac{\sigma^2}{2}\Delta s_t\right]dt.
\]
Integration by parts using the Fokker--Planck equation shows
\[
 \mathcal L_{\rm implicit}(s)-\mathcal L_{\rm implicit}(s^*)
 =\frac12\int_0^1\E_{p_t}
   \|\nabla s_t-
     \nabla s_t^*\|^2dt.
\]
The state-cost term in \eqref{eq:GSBM-main} is constant while $p_t$ is fixed, so this same gradient
field is the unique Stage-1 minimizer of the full generalized objective.

If the prescribed path is represented as a mixture of endpoint-conditioned paths
$p_t=\int p_{0,1}(dx_0,dx_1)p_{t\mid0,1}(\cdot\mid x_0,x_1)$ with conditional drift
$u_{t\mid0,1}$, then the explicit matching objective
\[
 \mathcal L_{\rm explicit}(u)
 =\frac12\int_0^1\E
 \|u_t(X_t)-u_{t\mid0,1}(X_t\mid X_0,X_1)\|^2dt
\]
is minimized by the conditional expectation of the bridge drift given $X_t$.  This Markovian
projection produces the same marginal path.

\section{Stage 2: conditional stochastic optimal control}
Now fix the endpoint coupling $p_{0,1}$ induced by the current diffusion and optimize the interior
marginals.  Disintegration and Fubini give
\[
 \mathcal J(u)
 =\int p_{0,1}(dx_0,dx_1)
 \int_0^1\E_{p_{t\mid0,1}}
 \left[\frac12\|u_{t\mid0,1}(X_t)\|^2+V_t(X_t)\right]dt.
\]
The endpoint pairs do not interact in this expression.  Therefore Stage 2 factorizes into independent
conditional control problems:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:GSBM-condSOC}
\begin{aligned}
 \inf_{u_{\cdot\mid0,1}}\quad&
 \int_0^1\E
 \left[\frac12\|u_t(X_t\mid x_0,x_1)\|^2+V_t(X_t)
\right]dt,\\
 \text{s.t.}\quad&
 dX_t=u_t(X_t\mid x_0,x_1)dt+\sigma\,dW_t,
 \qquad X_0=x_0, X_1=x_1.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Integrating the optimal conditional laws against $p_{0,1}$ yields the next marginal path.  This is the
mathematical basis of the alternating scheme.

\section{Quadratic state cost}
Let $V(x)=\alpha\|\sigma x\|^2$ with $\alpha,\sigma>0$ and put
$\eta=\sigma\sqrt{2\alpha}$.  The solution of \eqref{eq:GSBM-condSOC} is a Gaussian bridge
\[
 X_t^*\sim\mathcal N(c_tx_0+e_tx_1,gamma_t^2I),
\]
where
\begin{align*}
 c_t&=\frac{\sinh(\eta(1-t))}{\sinh\eta},\\
 e_t&=\cosh(\eta(1-t))-c_t\cosh\eta,\\
 \gamma_t&=\sigma
 \sqrt{\frac{\sinh(\eta(1-t))}{\eta} e_t}.
\end{align*}
To derive this, solve the linear-quadratic Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman equation with a terminal pinning
condition.  A quadratic ansatz for the value function yields Riccati equations for its quadratic and
linear coefficients.  Solving those equations gives the hyperbolic functions above; the covariance
then follows from the associated linear diffusion.  The endpoint identities
$c_0=1,e_0=0,gamma_0=0$ and $c_1=0,e_1=1,gamma_1=0$ verify the pinning.
As $\alpha\downarrow0$, hyperbolic-function expansions give
$c_t\to1-t$, $e_t\to t$, and $\gamma_t\to\sigma\sqrt{t(1-t)}$, the Brownian bridge.  Sending
$\sigma\downarrow0$ further gives straight-line interpolation.

\section{Path-integral representation}
Let $q(\cdot\mid x_0)$ be Brownian path measure with volatility $\sigma$ and initial point $x_0$.
The terminal condition $X_1=x_1$ can be represented by the singular terminal cost
$-\log\one_{\{x_1\}}(X_1)$.  The linearly solvable control identity gives the optimal path law
\[
 p^*(d\bar X\mid x_0,x_1)
 \propto
 \exp\left(-\frac1{\sigma^2}\int_0^1V_t(X_t)dt\right)
 \one_{\{x_1\}}(X_1)q(d\bar X\mid x_0).
\]
To sample efficiently, choose any pinned proposal law
$r(\cdot\mid x_0,x_1)\ll q(\cdot\mid x_0)$ generated by
$dX_t=v_t(X_t)dt+\sigma\,dW_t$.  Girsanov's theorem gives
\[
 \frac{dq}{dr}(\bar X)
 =\exp\left(-\int_0^1\frac{\|v_t(X_t)\|^2}{2\sigma^2}dt
             -\int_0^1\frac{v_t(X_t)^\top}{\sigma}dW_t\right).
\]
Therefore
\begin{equation}\label{eq:GSBM-path-integral}
 \frac{dp^*}{dr}(\bar X)
 =\frac1Z\exp\left[-\int_0^1
 \left(\frac{V_t(X_t)}{\sigma^2}
       +\frac{\|v_t(X_t)\|^2}{2\sigma^2}
       \right)dt
 -\int_0^1\frac{v_t(X_t)^\top}{\sigma}dW_t\right].
\end{equation}
Thus an approximate conditional bridge can be corrected by importance weighting.  When $V=0$,
the original formula reduces to Brownian motion conditioned on $X_1=x_1$.

\section{Monotonicity of alternating minimization}
Let $(p_t^n,u_t^n)$ be the feasible pair after the $n$th full iteration.  Stage 2 replaces each
conditional law by a minimizer of \eqref{eq:GSBM-condSOC} while keeping the endpoint coupling
$p_{0,1}^n$.  By Fubini,
\[
 \mathcal J(p^n,u^n)
 \ge\mathcal J(p^{n+1},u^n).
\]
Stage 1 then chooses, among drifts with marginals $p_t^{n+1}$, the unique minimum-kinetic-energy
drift.  Since the state-cost term depends only on the marginals,
\[
 \mathcal J(p^{n+1},u^n)
 \ge\mathcal J(p^{n+1},u^{n+1}).
\]
Hence
\[
 \mathcal J(p^n,u^n)\ge\mathcal J(p^{n+1},u^{n+1}).
\]
This is a local descent statement: exact execution of both stages never increases the generalized
bridge objective.

\section{Global optimum as a fixed point}
Let $(p_t^*,u_t^*)$ solve the generalized bridge problem.  Disintegrate its path law with respect to
the optimal endpoint coupling.  The forward--backward stochastic representation of the optimality
system shows that, for almost every endpoint pair, the conditional law solves
\eqref{eq:GSBM-condSOC}; otherwise replacing that conditional component would lower the global
objective.  Thus Stage 2 leaves $p^*$ unchanged.

The global optimal drift is a gradient field.  Since Stage 1 returns the unique gradient drift of least
kinetic energy compatible with $p_t^*$, implicit matching returns $u^*$.  The explicit matching
operator is the reverse-KL Markovian projection of the reciprocal mixture of the optimal conditional
bridges.  Because the globally optimal process is already Markov, this projection also returns
$u^*$.  Therefore every global optimum is a fixed point of the exact alternating scheme.
