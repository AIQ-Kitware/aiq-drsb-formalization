\section{What is complete}
Given a fixed multiplier and finite nonzero normalizing masses, Lean constructs a probability kernel pointwise by inverse-mass scaling, proves proportionality to the unnormalized Gibbs measure, and identifies it with Mathlib's tilted measure.  The Gibbs variational identity proves that this tilt exactly attains the inner entropy-penalized supremum.

\section{What is not yet derived}
\begin{comparison}

Budget feasibility & Not proved for the fixed tilt.\\
Complementary slackness & Not proved.\\
Primal attainment & Not proved.\\
\end{comparison}

\section{Correct reading}
This is a complete measure-normalization and inner-Gibbs theorem.  It must not be cited alone as existence of a worst-case distribution for the outer Sinkhorn-DRO problem.
