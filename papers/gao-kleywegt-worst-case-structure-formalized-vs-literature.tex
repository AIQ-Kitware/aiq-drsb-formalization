\section{What is constructed}
Given explicit measurable pointwise minimizer maps, a mixing weight, a transport-budget inequality, regularity sufficient to compute expectations, and an attainment-value edge, Lean constructs the mixture law, proves it is a probability measure, builds an explicit coupling to the nominal law, proves ambiguity-ball membership, computes its expectation, and proves the desired optimal-value equality or inequality.

The empirical theorem similarly constructs the weighted finite Dirac sum and its explicit transport plan.

\section{Nonclaims}
The declarations do not prove that a worst-case distribution exists under the paper's primitive assumptions, do not prove the source iff criterion for existence, and do not derive the two maps from the minimizing-radius analysis.  They should be read as verified constructive assembly theorems and useful targets for a later measurable-selection and attainment campaign.
